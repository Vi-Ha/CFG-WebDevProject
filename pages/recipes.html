Mushroom Risotto

Mushroom Risotto

Ingredients for 4 people
  • 2 shallots, thinly sliced
  • 350gr Arborio rice
  • 100gr dried Porcini Mushroom
  • 1lt chicken stock
  • 250ml white wine
  • 100gr butter
  • 3 tablespoons tickened cream
  • 5 tablespoons olive oil
  • Parmigiano cheese
  • Parsley
Preparation
  1. Soak dried porcini mushrooms in lukewarm water for 1-2 hours
  2. Prepare broth bringing 1lt water to a boil and adding chicken stock cubes, set broth aside and keep warm
  3. In a large pan, brown the sahllots in 5 tablespoons olive oil until golden. Add the rice and toast in oil and shallots mixture for 3 minutes, then add white wine and wait for it to evaporate completely.
  4. Start adding one ladle of chicken stock to the rice pan and mix well until the rice has absorbed the stock. Keep adding one ladle of stock at a time until all stock has been used or until the rice is cooked. Turn off fire.
  5. Add in the butter and thickened crean and mix well. Finally mix in Parmigiano and add parsly for garnish.
  6. Serve hot (with a nice glass of white wine hehe) and add more Parmigiano if you love cheese like me!
Singapore Fried Noodles

Singapore Fried Noodles

Ingredients
  • 1 package rice noodles
  • 250g lb Char Siu (Cantonese BBQ pork) or substitute meat
  • 250g shrimp
  • 4 eggs, beaten
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 medium onion sliced (or peppers/scallions)
  • 1 tbsp yellow curry
  • 1 tbsp turmeric
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • Salt, soy sauce, rice wine to taste
Preparation
  1. Follow instruction on the package for preparing the rice noodles
  2. Peel and devein the shrimp, season with salt and pepper
  3. Slice the char siu into strips
  4. In a large wok (or pan), cook the eggs as you would scramble eggs, and then loosely break them up into smaller pieces. Remove from wok into a separate bowl.
  5. Put the wok on high heat, add some of the oil, then stir-fry the garlic and onions. As that starts smoking/turning brown, continue to mix around every 30s. Then add the shrimp. Continue to stir-fry until shrimp is almost cooked. Then add in the char siu. Continue to stir-fry. If it gets a little dry, add in some rice wine and soy sauce. Add the eggs back in.
  6. Add the rice noodles into the mix. Add scissors to roughly cut the noodles into smaller pieces. Stir-fry all of it together, adding in the curry, turmeric and season with salt/soy sauce as needed.
Notes

Despite its name translation as "Singapore Fried Noodles", this is a classic found in Hong Kong "cha chan tangs" (tea restaurants) and not associated as part of Singaporean cuisine.

Tiramisu

Tiramisu

Ingredients
  • 250g Mascarpone cheese
  • 60g Granulated sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 bag of ladyfingers (about 24 pieces)
  • Unsweetened cocoa powder for garnish
  • 200mL Cold coffee
  • 1 tablespoons of rum
Preparing the Custard
  1. In a large bowl, whisk the egg yolks with half the sugar until pale and doubled in volume, 3 to 5 minutes. Add the mascarpone in 2 or 3 additions, whisking well to combine.
  2. In a clean bowl, whip the egg whites and remaining sugar to soft peaks. Fold the egg whites into the mascarpone mixture in two or three additions.
Preparing the Cake
  1. In a 15cm round pan or plate with a border, quickly dip each individual ladyfinger in the coffe and liquor mixture and arrange them very tightly in the pan. Spread half of the custard on top of the ladyfingers, arrange another layer of the ladyfingers and spread the remaning custard.
  2. Refrigerate for at least 6 hours. Just before serving, dust the top with cocoa powder and cover the edge with more ladyfingers!
Carott Cake

Carott cake

Ingredients (8-12 slices)
For the cake:
  • 200 grams plain flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon bicarbonate of soda
  • 2 teaspoons ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt
  • 175g soft light brown sugar
  • 2 large eggs (at room temperature)
  • 200mL vegetable oil (plus more for greasing)
  • 100g carrots (peeled and coarsely grated)
  • 100g walnut pieces (roughly chopped or crumbled)
For the icing:
  • 100g unsalted butter (soft)
  • 100g icing sugar (sieved if lumpy)
  • 1 teaspoon cornflour
  • 100g full-fat cream cheese (fridge-cold)
  • 1x15mL tablespoon fresh ginger (coarsely grated)
To decorate:
  • 25g walnut pieces (roughly chopped or crumbled)
  • 25g crystallised ginger (chopped)
Preparation

You will need 1 x 20cm springform cake tin.

  1. Preheat the oven to 170°C/150°C Fan and grease the sides and line the base of your springform cake tin with baking parchment.
  2. Put the flour, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, ground ginger and salt into a bowl and fork well to mix thoroughly.
  3. Beat the sugar, eggs and oil in another large bowl until they are completely mixed together, then gradually add the flour mixture, scraping the bowl you're beating them in to recue and incorporate any flour clinging to the edges. At this stage, the mixture may seem alarmingly stiff, but the carrots will loosen it up. So, beat in the carrots and then fold in the 100g/1 cup of prepared walnuts and 75g of crystallised ginger, until everything is evenly combind.
  4. Spoon and scrape into the prepared tin. Don't worry if it looks as if you haven't got nearly batter, as the cake will rise well as it bakes. Smooth the top and pop in the oven (this is when to make the icing, see step 5) for 45-55 minutes. When it's ready, the cake will be set and golden brown on top, beginning to shrink away from the edges of the tin and a cake tester will come out with just a few crumbs stuck to it. Transfer to a wire rack and leave to cool in its tin.
  5. As soon as the cake's in the oven, get on with the icing. Beat the butter and icing sugar together and when creamily combined, beat in the cornflour, followed by half the cream cheese. Once that's incorporated, beat in the remaining half. Be careful at all times not to over-beat or the icing will get too runny. Starting with the grated ginger on a plate, get out a piece of kitchen roll and moving quickly, spoon the grated ginger into the centre, bring up the edges of the paper, holding them together to form a little swag bag, and press on it over the bowl to squeeze out the intense ginger juice. Beat this into the frosting in its bowl. Cover with cling film and refrigerate
  6. When the cake is completely cold, take the icing out of the fridge for 20 minutes, by which time it will have softened to a still thick but spreadable consistency. Beat briefly to help this along, and make sure it's smooth. Unclip and release the cake from its tin, unmoulding it, amd sit it on a cake stand or plate. Spread the frosting on top, swirling it a little, then sprinkle the chopped walnuts and ginger on top.

Baked Camembert

Baked Camembert

Ingredients
  • 1 camembert (or brie if you prefer)
  • 1 garlic clove, peeled and sliced
  • 1/2 teaspoon fresh thyme leaves (or rosemary)
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 1 baguette
Preparation
  1. Preheat the oven to 200°C/180°C Fan. Remoe the camembert from its wooden box and discard any wax paper packaging. Place a 25cm square of foil in the box and place the camembert inside.
  2. Pierce the top of the camembert with the tip of a knife and push in the slices of garlic. Sprinkle the thyme leaves over the top. Loosely scrunch the foil up over the cheese.
  3. Bake for 15 minutes until the cheese has risen. You can also bake at the same time the baguette until the bread is crisp.
  4. Once cooked, drizzle a little honey on top and and dip in the hot baguette.
